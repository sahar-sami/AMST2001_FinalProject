Today, Cornell confers four types of undergraduate/baccalaureate degrees: Bachelor of Arts (B.A.), Bachelor of Science (B.S.), Bachelor of Architecture (B.Arch), and Bachelor of Fine Arts (B.F.A.). How have these offerings changed over time?

Methodology and Credits

I collected this data by going through volumes of the Cornell University Register spanning years 1869 to 1924. For each volume, I went through the section detailing the degrees conferred at Commencement and counted the number of records. I chose to focus on undergraduate degrees because they had more solid categories, and also chose to leave out undergraduate degrees that were around for no more than a few years.
